# journal

I decided to make an audio amplifier that fits on a keychain! First i started looking for suitable amplifier chips because no way was i gonna make one small enough. I found the lm326, which seemed like a good contender.

I then referenced the chip's datasheet for the recommended supporting components. I also found on Mouser some headphone jacks, 9v battery connector, etc.

Then, I laid out everything on the schematic:
<img width="837" height="519" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/baa05414-1426-4afb-b789-610e5e39641b" />

Afterwards, I had to assign footprints, and also look on Mouser for some of the passives to get some compatible components, and updated the BOM.

<img width="1349" height="447" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/d4e49a41-fcfb-4f3d-adaf-9904555c249e" />

Then, I converted the schematic to PCB, and laid everything out. I had to pay special attention to make sure everything was as small as possible, and got it to about 1.75x1.25 inches, which is pretty small!
Then followed the routing of all the wires, which required some repositioning of the components. I probably repositioned everything 2-3 times.

<img width="1066" height="660" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/0c4e6dec-0f28-4782-8dd9-077ae1b34d37" />

and we're done!
